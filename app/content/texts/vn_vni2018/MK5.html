5
1 Và they came over unto the other side of the sea, into the country of the Gadarenes. 2 Và when he was come out of the ship, tức thì there met him out of the tombs a man with an unclean spirit, 3 Who had his dwelling among the tombs; Và không ai could bind him, no, not with chains: 4 Because that he had been often bound with fetters Và chains, Và the chains had been plucked asunder by him, Và the fetters broken in pieces: neither could any man tame him. 5 Và always, night Và day, he was in the mountains, Và in the tombs, crying, Và cutting himself with stones. 6 But when he saw ĐCGiê-su afar off, he ran Và worshipped him, 7 Và cried with a loud voice, và nói, What have I to do with thee, ĐCGiê-su, thou Son of the most high ĐCT? I adjure thee by God, that thou torment me not. 8 For he said unto him, Come out of the man, thou unclean spirit. 9 Và he asked him, What is thy name? Và he answered, saying, My name is Legion: for we are many. 10 Và he besought him much that he would not send them away out of the country. 11 Now there was there nigh unto the mountains a great herd of swine feeding. 12 Và all the devils besought him, saying, Send us into the swine, that we may enter into them. 13 Và forthwith ĐCGiê-su gave them leave. Và the unclean spirits went out, Và entered into the swine: Và the herd ran violently down a steep place into the sea, (they were about two thousand;) Và were choked in the sea. 14 Và they that fed the swine fled, Và told it in the city, Và in the country. Và they went out to see what it was that was done. 15 Và they come to ĐCGiê-su, Và see him that was possessed with the devil, Và had the legion, sitting, Và clothed, Và in his right mind: Và they were afraid. 16 Và they that saw it told them how it befell to him that was possessed with the devil, Và also concerning the swine. 17 Và they began to pray him to depart out of their coasts. 18 Và when he was come into the ship, he that had been possessed with the devil prayed him that he might be with him. 19 Howbeit ĐCGiê-su suffered him not, but saith unto him, Go home to thy friends, Và tell them how great things the Lord hath done for thee, Và hath had compassion on thee. 20 Và he departed, Và began to publish in Decapolis how great things ĐCGiê-su had done for him: Và all men did marvel. 21 Và when ĐCGiê-su was passed over again by ship unto the other side, much people gathered unto him: Và he was nigh unto the sea. 22 Và, kìa, there cometh một of the rulers of the nhà hội, Jairus by name; Và when he saw him, he fell at his feet, 23 Và besought him greatly, saying, My little daughter lieth at the point of death: I pray thee, come Và lay thy hands on her, that she may be healed; Và she shall live. 24 Và Giê-su went with him; Và much people followed him, Và thronged him. 25 Và a certain woman, which had an issue of blood mười hai years, 26 Và had suffered many things of many physicians, Và had spent all that she had, Và was nothing bettered, but rather grew worse, 27 When she had heard of ĐCGiê-su, đến in the press behind, Và touched his garment. 28 For she said, nếu I may touch but his clothes, I shall be whole. 29 Và tức thì the fountain of her blood was dried up; Và she felt in her body that she was healed of that plague. 30 Và ĐCGiê-su, tức thì knowing in himself that virtue had gone out of him, turned him about in the press, và nói, Who touched my clothes? 31 Và his disciples said unto him, Thou seest đám đông thronging thee, Và sayest thou, Who touched me? 32 Và he looked round about to see her that had done this thing. 33 But the woman fearing Và trembling, knowing what was done in her, đến Và fell down before him, Và told him all the truth. 34 Và he said unto her, Daughter, thy faith hath made thee whole; go in peace, Và be whole of thy plague. 35 While he yet spake, there came from the ruler of the nhà hội's house certain which said, Thy daughter is dead: why troublest thou the Master any further? 36 As soon as ĐCGiê-su heard the word that was spoken, he saith unto the ruler of the nhà hội, Be not afraid, only believe. 37 Và he suffered không ai to follow him, save Phi-e-rơ, Và Gia-cơ, Và Giăng the brother of Gia-cơ. 38 Và he cometh to nhà of the ruler of the nhà hội, Và seeth the tumult, Và them that wept Và wailed greatly. 39 Và when he was come in, he saith unto them, Why make ye this ado, Và weep? the damsel is not dead, but sleepeth. 40 Và they laughed him to scorn. But when he had put them all out, he taketh the father Và the mother of the damsel, Và them that were with him, Và entereth in where the damsel was lying. 41 Và he took the damsel by the hand, và nói unto her, Talitha cumi; which is, being interpreted, Damsel, I say unto thee, Hãy chổi dậy. 42 Và tức thì the damsel arose, Và walked; for she was of the age of mười hai years. Và they were astonished with a great astonishment. 43 Và he charged them straitly that không ai should know it; Và commanded that something should be given her to eat.
