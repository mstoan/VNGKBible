23
1 Và Phao-lô, earnestly kìaing the council, nói, Men Và anh em, I have lived in all good conscience before God until this day. 2 Và thầy thượng phẩm Ananias commanded them that stood by him to smite him on the mouth. 3 Then nói Phao-lô unto him, ĐCT shall smite thee, thou whited wall: for sittest thou to judge me after the law, Và commandest me to be smitten contrary to the law? 4 Và they that stood by nói, Revilest thou God's high priest? 5 Then nói Phao-lô, I wist not, anh em, that he was thầy thượng phẩm: for it is written, Thou shalt not speak evil of the ruler of thy people. 6 Nhưng when Paul perceived that the one part were Sadducees, Và the other những người Pha-ri-si, he cried out in the council, Men Và anh em, I am a người Pha-ri-si, the son of a người Pha-ri-si: of the hy vọng Và resurrection of the dead I am called in question. 7 Và when he had so nói, there arose a dissension between the những người Pha-ri-si Và the Sadducees: Và đám đông was divided. 8 For the Sadducees say that there is no resurrection, neither angel, nor spirit: Nhưng the những người Pha-ri-si confess both. 9 Và there arose a great cry: Và những thầy chép luật that were of the những người Pha-ri-si' part arose, Và strove, Nói rằng, We find no evil in this man: Nhưng nếu a spirit hay an angel hath spoken to him, let us not fight against God. 10 Và when there arose a great dissension, the chief captain, fearing lest Phao-lô should have been pulled in pieces of them, commanded quân lính to go down, Và to take him by force from among them, Và to bring him into the castle. 11 Và the night following the Lord stood by him, và nói, Be of good cheer, Phao-lô: for as thou hast testified of me in Giê-ru-sa-lem, so must thou bear witness also at Rome. 12 Và when it was day, certain of the Jews banded together, Và bound themselves under a curse, saying that they would neither eat nor drink till they had killed Phao-lô. 13 Và they were more than forty which had made this conspiracy. 14 Và they đến to các thầy tế lễ Và elders, và nói, We have bound ourselves under a great curse, that we will eat nothing until we have slain Phao-lô. 15 bây giờ therefore ye with the council signify to the chief captain that he bring him down unto you to morrow, as though ye would enquire something more perfectly concerning him: Và we, or ever he come near, are ready to kill him. 16 Và when Paul's sister's son heard of their lying in wait, he went Và entered into the castle, Và told Phao-lô. 17 Then Phao-lô called một of the centurions unto him, và nói, Bring this young man unto the chief captain: for he hath a certain thing to tell him. 18 So he took him, Và brought him to the chief captain, Và nói, Phao-lô the prisoner called me unto him, Và prayed me to bring this young man unto thee, who hath something to say unto thee. 19 Then the chief captain took him by the hand, Và went with him aside privately, Và asked him, What is that thou hast to tell me? 20 Và he nói, The Jews have agreed to desire thee that thou wouldest bring down Phao-lô to morrow into the council, as though they would enquire somewhat of him more perfectly. 21 Nhưng do not thou yield unto them: for there lie in wait for him of them more than forty men, which have bound themselves with an oath, that they will neither eat nor drink till they have killed him: Và bây giờ are they ready, looking for a promise from thee. 22 So the chief captain then let the young man depart, Và charged him, See thou tell không ai that thou hast shewed these things to me. 23 Và he called unto him two centurions, Nói rằng, Make ready two hundred soldiers to go to Cæsarea, Và horsemen threescore and ten, Và spearmen two hundred, at the third hour of the night; 24 Và provide them beasts, that they may set Phao-lô on, Và bring him safe unto Felix thống đốc. 25 Và he wrote a letter after this manner: 26 Claudius Lysias unto the most excellent governor Felix sendeth greeting. 27 This man was taken of the Jews, Và should have been killed of them: then came I with an army, Và rescued him, having understood that he was a Roman. 28 Và when I would have known the cause wherefore they accused him, I brought him forth into their council: 29 Whom I perceived to be accused of questions of their law, Nhưng to have nothing laid to his charge worthy of death hay of bonds. 30 Và when it was told me how that the Jews laid wait for the man, I sent tức thì to thee, Và gave commandment to his accusers also to say before thee what they had against him. Farewell. 31 Then quân lính, as it was commanded them, took Phao-lô, Và brought him by night to Antipatris. 32 On the morrow they left the horsemen to go with him, Và returned to the castle: 33 Who, when they came to Cæsarea, Và delivered the epistle to thống đốc, presented Phao-lô also before him. 34 Và when thống đốc had read the letter, he asked of what province he was. Và when he understood that he was of Cilicia; 35 I will hear thee, nói he, when thine accusers are also come. Và he commanded him to be kept in Hê-rốt's judgment hall.
