27
1  Và when it was determined that we should sail into Italy, they delivered Phao-lô Và certain other prisoners unto one named Julius, một đội trưởng of Âu-gút-tơ' band. 2  Và entering into a ship of Adramyttium, we launched, meaning to sail by the coasts of Asia; one Aristarchus, a Macedonian of Thessalonica, being với us. 3  Và the next day we touched at Si-đôn. Và Julius courteously entreated Phao-lô, Và gave him liberty to go unto his friends to refresh himself. 4  Và when we had launched from thence, we sailed under Cyprus, because the winds were contrary. 5  Và when we had sailed over biển of Cilicia Và Pamphylia, we came to Myra, a city of Lycia. 6  Và there the centurion found a ship of Alexandria sailing into Italy; Và he put us therein. 7  Và when we had sailed slowly many days, Và scarce were come over against Cnidus, the wind không suffering us, we sailed under Crete, over against Salmone; 8  Và, hardly passing it, đến unto a place which is called The fair havens; nigh whereunto was the city of Lasea. 9  bây giờ when much time was spent, Và when sailing was bây giờ dangerous, because the fast was bây giờ already past, Phao-lô admonished them, 10  Và nói unto them, Sirs, I perceive rằng this voyage will be với hurt andmuch damage, không only of the lading Và ship, Nhưng also of our lives. 11  Nevertheless the centurion tin the master Và the owner of the ship, more than those things which were spoken by Phao-lô. 12  Và because the haven was not commodious to winter in, the more part advised to depart thence also, if by any means they might attain to Phenice, Và there to winter; which is an haven of Crete, Và lieth toward the south west Và north west. 13  Và when the south wind blew softly, supposing that they had obtained their purpose, loosing thence, they sailed close by Crete. 14  Nhưng không long after there arose against it a tempestuous wind, called Euroclydon. 15  Và when the ship was caught, Và could không bear up into the wind, we let her drive. 16  Và running under a certain island which is called Clauda, we had much work to come by the boat: 17  Which when they had taken up, they used helps, undergirding the ship; Và, fearing lest they should fall into the quicksands, strake sail, Và so were driven. 18  Và we being exceedingly tossed with a tempest, the next day they lightened the ship; 19  Và the third day we cast out with our own hands the tackling of the ship. 20  Và when neither sun nor stars in many days appeared, Và no small tempest lay on us, all hy vọng that we should được cứu was then taken away. 21  Nhưng after long abstinence Phao-lô stood forth in the midst of them, và nói, Sirs, ye should have hearkened unto me, Và không have loosed từ Crete, Và to have gained this harm Và loss. 22  Và bây giờ I exhort you to be of good cheer: for there shall be no loss of any man's sự sống among you, Nhưng of the ship. 23  For there stood by me this night thiên sứ của ĐCT, whose I am, Và whom I serve, 24  Nói rằng, Fear không, Phao-lô; thou must be brought before Sê-sa: Và, lo, ĐCT hath given thee all them that sail với thee. 25  Wherefore, sirs, be of good cheer: for I believe ĐCT, rằng it shall be even as it was told me. 26  Howbeit we must be cast upon a certain island. 27  Nhưng when the mười bốnth night was come, as we were driven up and down in Adria, about midnight the shipmen deemed that they drew near to some country; 28  Và sounded, Và found it hai mươi fathoms: Và when they had gone a little further, they sounded again, Và found it mười lăm fathoms. 29  Then fearing lest we should have fallen upon rocks, they cast bốn anchors out of the stern, Và wished for the day. 30  Và as the shipmen were about to flee out of the ship, when they had let down the boat into biển, under colour as though they would have cast anchors out of the foreship, 31  Phao-lô nói to the centurion Và to quân lính, Except these abide in the ship, ye cannot được cứu. 32  Then quân lính cut off the ropes of the boat, Và let her fall off. 33  Và while the day was coming on, Phao-lô besought them all to take meat, Nói rằng, This day is the mười bốnth day that ye have tarried Và continued fasting, having taken nothing. 34  Wherefore I pray you to take some meat: for this is for your health: for there shall không an hair fall từ the head of any of you. 35  Và when he had thus spoken, he took bánh, Và gave thanks to God in presence of them all: Và when he had broken it, he began để ăn. 36  Then were they all of good cheer, Và they also took some meat. 37  Và we were in all in the ship hai trăm threescore and sixteen souls. 38  Và when they had eaten enough, they lightened the ship, Và cast out the wheat into biển. 39  Và when it was day, they knew không the land: Nhưng they discovered a certain creek với a shore, into the which they were minded, nếu it were possible, to thrust in the ship. 40  Và when they had taken up the anchors, they committed themselves unto biển, Và loosed the rudder bands, Và hoised up the mainsail to the wind, Và made toward shore. 41  Và falling into a place where two seas met, they ran the ship aground; Và the forepart stuck fast, Và remained unmoveable, Nhưng the hinder part was broken với the violence of the waves. 42  Và quân lính' counsel was to giết the prisoners, lest any of them should swim out, Và escape. 43  Nhưng the centurion, willing to save Phao-lô, kept them từ their purpose; Và commanded that they which could swim should cast themselves first into biển, Và get to land: 44  Và the rest, some on boards, Và some on broken pieces of the ship. Và so xảy đến, that they escaped all safe to land.
